Signs You Need Brake Repair

Don't wait until it's too late. Bring your vehicle to our Nashville shop if you notice:

  • Squealing or squeaking when you apply the brakes
  • Grinding noise (metal-on-metal contact)
  • Brake warning light on your dashboard
  • Soft or spongy brake pedal
  • Vehicle pulls to one side when braking
  • Vibration or pulsation in the brake pedal
  • Longer stopping distances than normal

Frequently Asked Questions - Brake Repair Nashville

How much does brake repair cost in Nashville?

Brake pad replacement at Majesty Auto Repair typically costs between $150-$300 per axle, depending on your vehicle. Rotor replacement adds $100-$200 per axle. We offer free brake inspections so you know exactly what you need before any work begins.

How long does brake repair take?

Most brake pad replacements take 1-2 hours. If you need rotors replaced or resurfaced, plan for 2-3 hours. We work efficiently to get you back on Nashville roads safely and quickly.

Why are my brakes squealing?

Squealing brakes usually indicate worn brake pads. The built-in wear indicators create that high-pitched noise to alert you it's time for service. Don't ignore it - bring your car to Majesty Auto Repair for a free inspection.

What does it mean when my brakes are grinding?

Grinding brakes are a serious warning sign. This usually means your brake pads are completely worn and metal is grinding against metal, which can damage your rotors. Visit our Nashville shop immediately for an inspection.

What does the brake warning light mean?

The brake warning light can indicate low brake fluid, worn pads, or a problem with your brake system. It's important to have it checked right away. Stop by Majesty Auto Repair on Charlotte Pike for a free diagnostic.

Do I need new rotors or can they be resurfaced?

It depends on rotor thickness and condition. Our Nashville mechanics will measure your rotors during the free inspection and recommend resurfacing if there's enough material, or replacement if they're too thin or warped.
