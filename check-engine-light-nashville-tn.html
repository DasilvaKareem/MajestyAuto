Common Causes of Check Engine Light

The check engine light can be triggered by hundreds of different issues. Here are the most common ones we see at our Nashville shop:

  • Loose or damaged gas cap - The simplest fix! Check this first.
  • Oxygen sensor failure - Affects fuel efficiency and emissions
  • Catalytic converter problems - Can be expensive if ignored
  • Mass airflow sensor - Affects engine performance
  • Spark plugs or ignition coils - Cause misfires
  • Evaporative emission system (EVAP) leak - Fuel vapor issues
  • Thermostat - Cooling system problems
  • EGR valve - Exhaust gas recirculation issues

Check Engine Light: Steady vs. Flashing

Steady Light

A steady check engine light indicates a problem that should be addressed soon, but you can usually continue driving to get to our shop. Don't ignore it - the issue won't fix itself and could get worse.

Flashing Light

A flashing check engine light is urgent. This typically indicates an engine misfire. Continued driving could damage your catalytic converter (expensive!) or cause other serious problems. Get to a safe location and call us.

Frequently Asked Questions - Check Engine Light Nashville

How much does check engine light diagnostics cost in Nashville?

Check engine light diagnostics at Majesty Auto Repair typically costs $50-$100. This includes reading the codes, interpreting them, and providing you with a clear explanation of what's wrong and what repairs are needed.

Can I drive with my check engine light on?

If the light is steady (not flashing), you can usually drive short distances to get to a repair shop. However, if the light is flashing, pull over safely and call for assistance - this indicates a serious problem that could cause engine damage.

What are common causes of check engine light?

Common causes include loose gas cap, oxygen sensor issues, catalytic converter problems, mass airflow sensor, spark plugs, or evaporative emission system leaks. Our Nashville mechanics will diagnose the exact cause for you.

How long does check engine light diagnosis take?

A basic code read takes just a few minutes. Full diagnosis to pinpoint the exact cause typically takes 30-60 minutes. We'll explain everything we find before recommending any repairs.

Will the check engine light reset itself?

Sometimes, if the underlying problem is resolved (like tightening a loose gas cap), the light may turn off after several driving cycles. However, we recommend getting it diagnosed to ensure there's no underlying issue that could cause bigger problems.

My check engine light is flashing - what should I do?

A flashing check engine light indicates a serious problem, usually an engine misfire that could damage your catalytic converter. Reduce speed, avoid heavy acceleration, and get to our Nashville shop as soon as safely possible.
